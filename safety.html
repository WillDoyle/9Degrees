Safety In Bouldering

BOULDERING IS A FUN AND CHALLENGING SPORT, BUT NOT ENTIRELY WITHOUT RISKS.

We offer you the facilities to climb, however, you remain responsible for your own actions when climbing, falling or jumping off.

House Rules

  1. 1. Sign waiver and check in at front desk for each visit.
  2. 2. Obey staff instructions.
  3. 3. Attempt to climb down instead of jumping.
  4. 4. Keep mats clear always.
  5. 5. Empty pockets and remove jewelry.
  6. 6. Maintain distance from other climbers.
  7. 7. Warm up before challenging climbs; stop if in pain.
  8. 8. Climbing shoes must be worn

Risks of Falling

  1. Always climb down using extra holds for grip.
  2. Land on both feet for a softer impact.
  3. Don't reach for holds if you're unsure or tired.
  4. Bend your knees, not your hips, to absorb impact.
  5. Avoid twisting while climbing.
  6. Learn to fall and roll from low heights.
  7. Check the area before starting to climb.


    8. Watch our safety video below for proper falling techniques!

Waiver

EVERY PERSON CLIMBING AT 9 DEGREES NEEDS TO SIGN A WAIVER ON THEIR FIRST VISIT. NO WAIVER, NO CLIMBING.

A signed waiver is valid across all 9 Degrees gyms so you will not need to re-sign when visiting a different 9 Degrees. For any climbers under the age of 18, a parent or legal guardian must also sign the form. You can read more about our under 18 guidelines here.

If you sign the waiver online please let our staff know so they can get you started quicker!
